Introduction

Welcome to Beijing, China! This site serves as a guide for trips in the Chinese capital city. Beijing has been the capital of China for over 800 years and is known for its rich history, culture, and modern development. From the Great Wall to the Forbidden City, there are countless attractions to explore. The city is also famous for its delicious cuisine, including Peking duck and various street foods. Getting around is easy with an extensive public transportation system, including subways and buses. For accommodation, there are options ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Whether you're here for business or leisure, Beijing has something to offer for everyone.

Forbidden City 紫禁城/故宫

The Forbidden City served as the imperial palace for over 500 years and is a symbol of traditional Chinese architecture.

  • Visit the Forbidden City
  • Visit the Jingshan Park

Jingshan Park 景山公园 is located right above the Forbidden City. You can get a panoramic view of the forbidden city from just a small hike.

Great Wall of China 长城

The Great Wall stretches across northern China and is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world.

  • Visit the Great Wall

Summer Palace 颐和园

The Summer Palace is a vast ensemble of lakes, gardens, and palaces used as a summer retreat by Qing emperors.

  • Explore the Summer Palace

Old Summer Palace 圆明园

Once known for its extensive collection of gardens and art, the Old Summer Palace was destroyed during the Second Opium War.

Temple of Heaven 天坛

The Temple of Heaven was where emperors worshipped heaven and prayed for good harvests.

  • Visit the Temple of Heaven

Hutong 胡同

Beijing's hutongs are narrow alleys that reflect the city's rich cultural history and daily life.

Bell & Drum Towers 钟鼓楼

These towers once kept time for the city and now offer panoramic views of historic Beijing neighborhoods.

Food

  • Peking Duck 北京烤鸭
  • Lamb Hot Pot 涮羊肉
  • Baodu 爆肚

Peking Duck 北京烤鸭

Peking Duck is a world-famous Beijing dish known for its crispy skin, tender meat, and rich flavors, traditionally served with thin pancakes, scallions, and hoisin sauce.

Lamb Hot Pot 涮羊肉

Lamb Hot Pot is a traditional Beijing winter dish where thinly sliced lamb is cooked in a boiling pot of broth at the table and eaten with sesame sauce, garlic, and a variety of vegetables.

Baodu 爆肚

Baodu is a classic Beijing street food made of quickly blanched beef or lamb tripe, often served with a savory dipping sauce made of sesame paste, vinegar, and chili oil.
